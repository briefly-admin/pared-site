## Newsletter #2 — Kitchen Edition

**Subject:** The case against your non-stick pan
**Preview text:** What's actually in your cookware matters.

---

Hi,

Let's talk about your kitchen.

Non-stick pans are coated with PTFE (Teflon), which breaks down and releases toxic fumes when heated above 500°F. Even at normal cooking temperatures, the coating degrades over time, and those microplastic particles end up in your food.

The alternative has existed for centuries: cast iron.

**This week's pick: Smithey No. 10 Cast Iron Skillet ($200)**

Hand-poured and polished in Charleston, South Carolina. No coatings, no chemicals — just iron. It gets more "non-stick" the more you use it. Your grandchildren will cook with this skillet.

Also worth noting: Smithey's craftsmanship is exceptional. The polished interior is silky smooth — not the bumpy texture you find on budget cast iron. This is heirloom quality.

→ See it on pared.shop

**Quick tip:** Season your cast iron with flaxseed oil for the hardest, most durable finish. Thin coats, 450°F, one hour. Repeat 3-4 times.
